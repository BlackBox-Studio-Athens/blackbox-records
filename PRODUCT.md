# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

BlackBox Records serves listeners, record buyers, artists, bands, promoters, press, and collaborators who want a direct view into the label's roster, releases, distro shelf, services, and contact paths. Public visitors are usually browsing music, checking a release, finding a band, buying a physical item, or deciding whether the label is a credible and human place to work with.

Internal operator surfaces such as stock operations are product surfaces by exception. Treat those task by task as work tools, but keep the public site and storefront under this default brand register.

## Product Purpose

BlackBox Records is a static Astro site for an Athens label and collective. It presents artists, releases, distro entries, news, services, and a native commerce path while preserving the label's direct community posture.

Success means the site feels label-operated and current, not like a generic ecommerce catalog. It should make the roster, music, physical formats, checkout flow, and practical service offers easy to understand without letting commerce or tooling own the identity.

## Brand Personality

Direct, collective, editorial.

The voice is practical and human: no polished agency distance, no inflated startup language, no fake scarcity. The label helps bands release music, tour, print merch, and handle physical formats under a collaborative umbrella.

The experience should feel monochrome, physical, independent, and music-first. It should suggest a record shelf, a gig wall, a distro table, and a working label office more than a marketplace dashboard.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like a generic ecommerce grid, SaaS landing page, polished corporate music platform, Shopify clone, marketplace catalog, or debug-heavy admin UI.

Avoid pastel ecommerce styling, neon music-app cliches, crypto/nightclub gradients, glassmorphism, decorative metric blocks, oversized repeated cards with icons, fake urgency, fake scarcity, and visual treatments that make bands feel like product inventory.

Do not let Stripe, BOX NOW, stock operations, or backend identifiers become the shopper-facing visual system.

## Design Principles

1. Keep the label in charge. Commerce, checkout, shipping, and services must sit inside the BlackBox editorial system.
2. Make physical music legible. Releases, distro formats, artist context, and listening actions should scan quickly without becoming marketplace-dense.
3. Preserve the shell. Navigation, overlays, and the persistent player are part of the experience model, not optional decoration.
4. Use restraint with intent. Monochrome is the default; accent color appears only where it clarifies a section or action.
5. Write like people work here. Copy should be clear, direct, and specific, with no corporate filler or fake hype.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target accessible, keyboard-usable public pages with meaningful headings, visible focus states, readable text contrast, and native or explicit labels for links, buttons, player controls, checkout controls, and form fields.

Respect reduced-motion preferences. Motion may support orientation and state changes, but it must not be required to understand navigation, checkout status, shipping selection, or music-player behavior.

**Gathered:** 2026-04-20
**Status:** Planning complete

<domain>
## Phase Boundary

Phase 5.1 is the architecture gate for the commerce domain. It freezes the entity model, source-of-truth split, IDs, mappings, and API contracts before storefront, D1, or Stripe implementation continues.

</domain>

<decisions>
## Implementation Decisions

### Domain defaults

- **D-01:** `Artist` is editorial only and not directly sellable.
- **D-02:** `Release` is editorial content that may map to one or more sellable variants.
- **D-03:** `DistroEntry` is editorial store content that may map to one or more sellable variants.
- **D-04:** `StoreItem` is the canonical storefront-facing identity derived from one content source: `release` or `distro`.
- **D-05:** `Variant` is the primary sellable unit for stock, checkout, Stripe mapping, and later order lines.
- **D-05.1:** `StoreItem` is browser-facing and may expose one or more `Variant` records beneath it.
- **D-05.2:** `Variant` is never authored directly in Astro collections.

### Source of truth

- **D-06:** Astro content collections own editorial content only.
- **D-07:** Stripe owns sellable commerce data needed for checkout.
- **D-08:** D1 owns operational state plus internal mappings.
- **D-08.1:** The projection layer derives `StoreItem` views from authoritative sources but does not become a fourth source of truth.

### Architecture boundaries

- **D-09:** The frontend consumes a stable `StoreItem` and `ItemAvailability` contract rather than talking directly to Stripe or D1.
- **D-10:** The Worker backend is the only component that talks to Stripe or D1.
- **D-11:** IDs, slugs, and mappings must be explicit before implementation.
- **D-12:** Backend interface names must follow the commerce ubiquitous language instead of generic technical labels.
- **D-13:** Shared backend contracts are authored in TypeScript and consumed through layered boundaries rather than direct framework-coupled modules.

</decisions>

<specifics>
## Specific Ideas

- Use a projection layer to derive shop-facing content from `releases` and `distro`.
- Keep editorial content out of D1 and keep operational state out of Astro collections.
- Treat current `fourthwall_url` fields as transitional external-shop handoff data, not as canonical native-commerce identity.
- Do not add temporary pricing, stock, or Stripe identity fields directly to the Astro content collections.
- Draft APIs before writing code so later phases can stay implementation-focused.

</specifics>
